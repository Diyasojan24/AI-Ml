Cats have five toes on their front paws, but only four on their back paws.
A group of cats is called a clowder.
Cats can jump up to six times their length.
Cats sleep for approximately 70% of their lives.
A cat's nose print is as unique as a human's fingerprint.
Cats can travel at approximately 31 mph (49 km) over a short distance.
Cats have a field of vision of about 200 degrees, compared to humans with 180 degrees.
Domestic cats purr at a frequency of 25 to 150 vibrations per second.
Cats have 32 muscles in each ear, allowing them to rotate their ears 180 degrees.
A cat's heart beats at a rate of 110 to 140 beats per minute, nearly twice as fast as a human's.
